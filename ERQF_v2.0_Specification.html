Fraud Probability Model — P(Fraud)

v2.0 · Time-Decay

Probability of fraud is computed using exponential time-decay weighting, giving recent scans significantly more influence than older ones. This captures trend direction — a company improving its fraud rate will see lower P(Fraud) than one with the same aggregate rate but worsening trend.

Primary Formula $$P(\text{Fraud}) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot f_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot n_i}$$
Where:
\( w_i = e^{-\lambda \cdot d_i} \) — exponential decay weight for day \(i\)
\( \lambda = 0.05 \) — decay constant (half-life ≈ 14 days)
\( d_i \) — number of days ago for day \(i\)
\( f_i = \text{counterfeit}_i + \text{suspicious}_i \times C_r \) — weighted fraud count per day
\( n_i \) — total scans on day \(i\)
\( D \) — number of days with scan data (up to 30)
Confirmation Rate $$C_r = \frac{\text{Critical Alerts} + \text{High Alerts}}{\text{Total Alerts}}$$

Trend Detection: Compare recent 7-day fraud rate vs prior 7-day rate:

\( R_{\text{recent}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{0..7}}{\text{total}_{0..7}} \),   \( R_{\text{older}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{8..14}}{\text{total}_{8..14}} \)

If \( R_{\text{recent}} > 1.2 \times R_{\text{older}} \) → Worsening (+1)
If \( R_{\text{recent}} < 0.8 \times R_{\text{older}} \) → Improving (−1)
Otherwise → Stable (0)
Fallback: If fewer than 3 days of daily data available, falls back to simple ratio: \( P(\text{Fraud}) = \frac{C_{30} + S_{30} \times C_r}{N_{30}} \)

Expected Revenue Loss — ERL

Quantifies direct revenue at risk from fraud-related product diversion, counterfeiting losses, and channel leakage. Adjusted by scan coverage ratio to extrapolate from observed to total estimated volume.

Primary Formula $$\text{ERL} = R_{\text{covered}} \times P(\text{Fraud}) \times (1 - r_{\text{recovery}})$$
Where:
\( R_{\text{covered}} = R_{\text{annual}} \times \text{Coverage} \) — revenue covered by scan network
\( \text{Coverage} = \min\!\left(\frac{\text{totalScans}}{\text{estimatedUnits} / 12},\ 1\right) \) — monthly scan penetration rate
\( r_{\text{recovery}} \) — expected recovery rate (default: 0.2 = 20%)
\( 1 - r_{\text{recovery}} \) = Severity factor

Expected Brand Impact — EBI

Models brand value at risk using a power-law trust decay (Brand Risk Factor) combined with a saturating incident escalation (1 − exponential CDF). Industry-specific β and k parameters from Risk Clusters control sensitivity.

Primary Formula $$\text{EBI} = \left[V_{\text{brand}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - \frac{T}{100}\right)^\beta}_{\text{Brand Risk Factor}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - e^{-k \cdot P(\text{Fraud})}\right)}_{\text{Incident Escalation}}\right] + \mathcal{W}_{\text{mcap}}$$
Market Cap Wipeout (Public Co. Only) $$\mathcal{W}_{\text{mcap}} = V_{\text{mcap}} \times \min(P(\text{Fraud}) \cdot \beta,\ 0.05)$$
Where:
\( V_{\text{brand}} \) — estimated brand value (USD)
\( V_{\text{mcap}} \) — market capitalization of public company
\( T \) — average trust score (0–100)
\( \beta \) — brand sensitivity exponent (from Risk Cluster, range 1.2–2.8)
\( k \) — incident escalation rate (from Risk Cluster, range 1.25–2.8)

BRF behavior: Higher β → brand more sensitive to trust erosion (luxury/pharma)
IE behavior: Saturating function — small fraud rates cause proportionally larger escalation
MCap behavior: Models 2%–5% cap wipeout during panic sell-offs

Regulatory Fine Exposure — RFE

v2.0 · Sigmoid

Estimates regulatory fine exposure using a sigmoid enforcement probability that replaces the previous 3-step cliff function, providing smooth risk transition without artificial discontinuities.

Primary Formula $$\text{RFE} = \text{WCRS} \times \bar{F}_{\text{industry}} \times \mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) \times N_{\text{nc}}$$
Compliance Risk Score $$\text{WCRS} = \frac{N_{\text{nc}} \times 1.0 + N_{\text{partial}} \times 0.5}{N_{\text{total}}}$$
Sigmoid Enforcement · v2.0 $$\mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) = 0.08 + \frac{0.87}{1 + e^{-12 \cdot (\text{WCRS} - 0.2)}}$$
Where:
\( \bar{F}_{\text{industry}} \) — average regulatory fine per incident (industry-specific, see §9)
\( N_{\text{nc}} \) — number of non-compliant records
\( N_{\text{partial}} \) — number of partially compliant records

Sigmoid parameters: L = 0.08 (floor), U = 0.95 (ceiling), midpoint = 0.2, steepness = 12
Behavior: Smooth transition from 8% → 95% enforcement probability as WCRS increases
v1.0 (deprecated): Step function — \(\mathcal{E} = 0.6\) if WCRS > 0.3, \(0.3\) if > 0.15, \(0.1\) otherwise. This created "cliff effects" at thresholds.

Supply Chain Risk Index — SCRI

v2.0 · Dynamic Weights

Composite risk index (0–1) combining four risk drivers with cluster-specific weights. Life-critical industries weight compliance higher, while luxury weights fraud/brand higher.

Primary Formula $$\text{SCRI} = \alpha_1 \cdot P(\text{Fraud}) + \alpha_2 \cdot \text{WCRS} + \alpha_3 \cdot G_{\max} + \alpha_4 \cdot \min(V \times 10,\ 1)$$
Where:
\( G_{\max} = \max(\text{avg\_fraud\_score per country}) \) — worst geographic risk
\( V = |r_{\text{current}} - r_{\text{previous}}| \) — fraud rate volatility (month-over-month)
\( \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4 \) — cluster-specific weights (see below)

Total Capital at Risk — TCAR

v2.0 · ρ-Based

Aggregates all risk components with correlation-based diversification using portfolio volatility theory. Since ERL and EBI share the same fraud driver, their correlation is high (ρ ≈ 0.7), reducing the diversification benefit compared to v1.0.

Primary Formula $$\text{TCAR} = \text{ERL} + \text{EBI} + \text{RFE} - \mathcal{D}$$
Diversification Benefit · v2.0 $$\mathcal{D} = (\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}) \times \left(1 - \sqrt{w_1^2 + w_2^2 + 2\rho \cdot w_1 \cdot w_2}\right)$$
Where:
\( w_1 = \frac{\text{ERL}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \),   \( w_2 = \frac{\text{EBI}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \)
\( \rho = 0.7 \) — correlation between revenue loss and brand damage

Numerical example: If ERL = EBI (equal split), then \( w_1 = w_2 = 0.5 \):
\( \sqrt{0.25 + 0.25 + 2 \times 0.7 \times 0.25} = \sqrt{0.85} = 0.922 \)
\( \mathcal{D} = (E+E) \times (1 - 0.922) = 7.8\% \) diversification benefit

v1.0 (deprecated): \( \mathcal{D} = 0.3 \times \min(\text{ERL}, \text{EBI}) \) — up to 30% benefit, overly optimistic

Scenario Modeling — 5-Point Envelope

v2.0 · Extended

Each scenario recalculates the entire ERQF pipeline with modified inputs. The 5-point envelope provides a comprehensive risk distribution for board-level decision making.

Scenario Recalculation $$\text{TCAR}_s = \text{ERL}_s + \text{EBI}_s + \text{RFE}_s - \mathcal{D}_s$$
\( \text{ERL}_s = R_{\text{covered}} \times \min(P \times m_f \times \tau,\ 1) \times (1 - r) \)
\( \text{EBI}_s = V_b \times (1 - \frac{T + \Delta T}{100})^{\beta \cdot \tau} \times (1 - e^{-k \cdot P \cdot m_f}) + \mathcal{W}_{\text{mcap}, s} \)
\( \text{RFE}_s = \text{WCRS} \times \bar{F} \times \mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS} \times m_e \times \tau) \times N_{\text{nc}} \times \tau \)
Where \( \tau \) = Fat-Tail Multiplier (default 1.0, Extreme 2.5)

🟢 Best0.6× (−40%)+5%0.3×Optimistic: strong anti-fraud program impact
🟢 Moderate0.8× (−20%)+3%0.5×Gradual improvement trajectory

95% Confidence Interval

Closed-form sensitivity analysis using the parameter distributions from Risk Clusters. TCAR is recalculated at ±1.96σ parameter shifts for β, k, and recovery rate.

Confidence Bounds $$\text{TCAR}_{95\%} = \left[\text{TCAR}(\theta_{\text{opt}}),\ \text{TCAR}(\theta_{\text{pes}})\right]$$
Optimistic bound (lower TCAR):
\( \beta_{\text{opt}} = \max(1.0,\ \bar{\beta} - 1.96\sigma_\beta) \),   \( k_{\text{opt}} = \max(0.5,\ \bar{k} - 1.96\sigma_k) \)
\( r_{\text{opt}} = \min(0.9,\ \bar{r} + 1.96\sigma_r) \) — higher recovery = lower loss

Pessimistic bound (higher TCAR):
\( \beta_{\text{pes}} = \min(3.5,\ \bar{\beta} + 1.96\sigma_\beta) \),   \( k_{\text{pes}} = \min(8.0,\ \bar{k} + 1.96\sigma_k) \)
\( r_{\text{pes}} = \max(0.2,\ \bar{r} - 1.96\sigma_r) \) — lower recovery = higher loss
Note: If custom β or k overrides are set by the tenant, CI is disabled (bounds = base TCAR) since the custom values represent the user's expert judgment, not a statistical distribution.

Per-BU Segmented Aggregation

For multi-business-unit organizations, ERQF computes risk at BU level then aggregates with cross-BU correlation and brand contagion effects.

Per-BU TCAR $$\text{TCAR}_{\text{bu}} = \text{ERL}_{\text{bu}} + \text{EBI}_{\text{bu}} + \text{RFE}_{\text{bu}}$$
Each BU uses its own:
• Revenue weight \( w_{\text{bu}} \) — fraction of total revenue
• Cluster parameters — BU can override β, k, avgFine or inherit from org
• P(Fraud) — computed from BU's product categories' scan data
• Coverage — \( \text{BU scans} / (\text{estimated units} \times w_{\text{bu}} / 12) \)
Group Aggregation with Contagion $$\text{TCAR}_{\text{group}} = \sum_i \text{TCAR}_i + \mathcal{C}_{\text{contagion}} - \mathcal{D}_{\text{group}}$$
Brand Contagion (Branded House only):
\( \mathcal{C} = \gamma \times \sum_i P(\text{Fraud})_i \times \left(V_{\text{brand}} - V_{\text{brand}} \times w_i\right) \times \text{IE}_i \)
\( \gamma \) — contagion factor (0 = no contagion, 1 = full contagion)

Group Diversification (Actuarial Covariance):
\( \mathcal{D}_{\text{group}} = (1 - \rho_{\text{cross}}) \cdot \mathcal{D}_{\text{cov}}(E_{\text{group}}, B_{\text{group}}) + (\text{TCAR}_{\text{raw}} \times 0.05) \)
\( \rho_{\text{cross}} \) — cross-BU correlation (default: 0.3)
