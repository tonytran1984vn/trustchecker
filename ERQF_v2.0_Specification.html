Fraud Probability Model ‚Äî P(Fraud)

v2.0 ¬∑ Time-Decay

Probability of fraud is computed using exponential time-decay weighting, giving recent scans significantly more influence than older ones. This captures trend direction ‚Äî a company improving its fraud rate will see lower P(Fraud) than one with the same aggregate rate but worsening trend.

Primary Formula $$P(\text{Fraud}) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot f_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot n_i}$$
Where:
\( w_i = e^{-\lambda \cdot d_i} \) ‚Äî exponential decay weight for day \(i\)
\( \lambda = 0.05 \) ‚Äî decay constant (half-life ‚âà 14 days)
\( d_i \) ‚Äî number of days ago for day \(i\)
\( f_i = \text{counterfeit}_i + \text{suspicious}_i \times C_r \) ‚Äî weighted fraud count per day
\( n_i \) ‚Äî total scans on day \(i\)
\( D \) ‚Äî number of days with scan data (up to 30)
Confirmation Rate $$C_r = \frac{\text{Critical Alerts} + \text{High Alerts}}{\text{Total Alerts}}$$

Trend Detection: Compare recent 7-day fraud rate vs prior 7-day rate:

\( R_{\text{recent}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{0..7}}{\text{total}_{0..7}} \),   \( R_{\text{older}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{8..14}}{\text{total}_{8..14}} \)

If \( R_{\text{recent}} > 1.2 \times R_{\text{older}} \) ‚Üí Worsening (+1)
If \( R_{\text{recent}} < 0.8 \times R_{\text{older}} \) ‚Üí Improving (‚àí1)
Otherwise ‚Üí Stable (0)
Fallback: If fewer than 3 days of daily data available, falls back to simple ratio: \( P(\text{Fraud}) = \frac{C_{30} + S_{30} \times C_r}{N_{30}} \)

Expected Revenue Loss ‚Äî ERL

Quantifies direct revenue at risk from fraud-related product diversion, counterfeiting losses, and channel leakage. Adjusted by scan coverage ratio to extrapolate from observed to total estimated volume.

Primary Formula $$\text{ERL} = R_{\text{covered}} \times P(\text{Fraud}) \times (1 - r_{\text{recovery}})$$
Where:
\( R_{\text{covered}} = R_{\text{annual}} \times \text{Coverage} \) ‚Äî revenue covered by scan network
\( \text{Coverage} = \min\!\left(\frac{\text{totalScans}}{\text{estimatedUnits} / 12},\ 1\right) \) ‚Äî monthly scan penetration rate
\( r_{\text{recovery}} \) ‚Äî expected recovery rate (default: 0.2 = 20%)
\( 1 - r_{\text{recovery}} \) = Severity factor

Expected Brand Impact ‚Äî EBI

Models brand value at risk using a power-law trust decay (Brand Risk Factor) combined with a saturating incident escalation (1 ‚àí exponential CDF). Industry-specific Œ≤ and k parameters from Risk Clusters control sensitivity.

Primary Formula $$\text{EBI} = V_{\text{brand}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - \frac{T}{100}\right)^\beta}_{\text{Brand Risk Factor}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - e^{-k \cdot P(\text{Fraud})}\right)}_{\text{Incident Escalation}}$$
Where:
\( V_{\text{brand}} \) ‚Äî estimated brand value (USD)
\( T \) ‚Äî average trust score (0‚Äì100)
\( \beta \) ‚Äî brand sensitivity exponent (from Risk Cluster, range 1.2‚Äì2.8)
\( k \) ‚Äî incident escalation rate (from Risk Cluster, range 1.25‚Äì2.8)

BRF behavior: Higher Œ≤ ‚Üí brand more sensitive to trust erosion (luxury/pharma)
IE behavior: Saturating function ‚Äî small fraud rates cause proportionally larger escalation

Regulatory Fine Exposure ‚Äî RFE

v2.0 ¬∑ Sigmoid

Estimates regulatory fine exposure using a sigmoid enforcement probability that replaces the previous 3-step cliff function, providing smooth risk transition without artificial discontinuities.

Primary Formula $$\text{RFE} = \text{WCRS} \times \bar{F}_{\text{industry}} \times \mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) \times N_{\text{nc}}$$
Compliance Risk Score $$\text{WCRS} = \frac{N_{\text{nc}} \times 1.0 + N_{\text{partial}} \times 0.5}{N_{\text{total}}}$$
Sigmoid Enforcement ¬∑ v2.0 $$\mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) = 0.08 + \frac{0.57}{1 + e^{-12 \cdot (\text{WCRS} - 0.2)}}$$
Where:
\( \bar{F}_{\text{industry}} \) ‚Äî average regulatory fine per incident (industry-specific, see ¬ß9)
\( N_{\text{nc}} \) ‚Äî number of non-compliant records
\( N_{\text{partial}} \) ‚Äî number of partially compliant records

Sigmoid parameters: L = 0.08 (floor), U = 0.65 (ceiling), midpoint = 0.2, steepness = 12
Behavior: Smooth transition from 8% ‚Üí 65% enforcement probability as WCRS increases
v1.0 (deprecated): Step function ‚Äî \(\mathcal{E} = 0.6\) if WCRS > 0.3, \(0.3\) if > 0.15, \(0.1\) otherwise. This created "cliff effects" at thresholds.

Supply Chain Risk Index ‚Äî SCRI

v2.0 ¬∑ Dynamic Weights

Composite risk index (0‚Äì1) combining four risk drivers with cluster-specific weights. Life-critical industries weight compliance higher, while luxury weights fraud/brand higher.

Primary Formula $$\text{SCRI} = \alpha_1 \cdot P(\text{Fraud}) + \alpha_2 \cdot \text{WCRS} + \alpha_3 \cdot G_{\max} + \alpha_4 \cdot \min(V \times 10,\ 1)$$
Where:
\( G_{\max} = \max(\text{avg\_fraud\_score per country}) \) ‚Äî worst geographic risk
\( V = |r_{\text{current}} - r_{\text{previous}}| \) ‚Äî fraud rate volatility (month-over-month)
\( \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4 \) ‚Äî cluster-specific weights (see below)

Total Capital at Risk ‚Äî TCAR

v2.0 ¬∑ œÅ-Based

Aggregates all risk components with correlation-based diversification using portfolio volatility theory. Since ERL and EBI share the same fraud driver, their correlation is high (œÅ ‚âà 0.7), reducing the diversification benefit compared to v1.0.

Primary Formula $$\text{TCAR} = \text{ERL} + \text{EBI} + \text{RFE} - \mathcal{D}$$
Diversification Benefit ¬∑ v2.0 $$\mathcal{D} = (\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}) \times \left(1 - \sqrt{w_1^2 + w_2^2 + 2\rho \cdot w_1 \cdot w_2}\right)$$
Where:
\( w_1 = \frac{\text{ERL}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \),   \( w_2 = \frac{\text{EBI}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \)
\( \rho = 0.7 \) ‚Äî correlation between revenue loss and brand damage

Numerical example: If ERL = EBI (equal split), then \( w_1 = w_2 = 0.5 \):
\( \sqrt{0.25 + 0.25 + 2 \times 0.7 \times 0.25} = \sqrt{0.85} = 0.922 \)
\( \mathcal{D} = (E+E) \times (1 - 0.922) = 7.8\% \) diversification benefit

v1.0 (deprecated): \( \mathcal{D} = 0.3 \times \min(\text{ERL}, \text{EBI}) \) ‚Äî up to 30% benefit, overly optimistic

Scenario Modeling ‚Äî 5-Point Envelope

v2.0 ¬∑ Extended

Each scenario recalculates the entire ERQF pipeline with modified inputs. The 5-point envelope provides a comprehensive risk distribution for board-level decision making.

Scenario Recalculation $$\text{TCAR}_s = \text{ERL}_s + \text{EBI}_s + \text{RFE}_s - \mathcal{D}_s$$
\( \text{ERL}_s = R_{\text{covered}} \times P(\text{Fraud}) \times m_f \times (1 - r) \)
\( \text{EBI}_s = V_b \times (1 - \frac{T + \Delta T}{100})^\beta \times (1 - e^{-k \cdot P \cdot m_f}) \)
\( \text{RFE}_s = \text{WCRS} \times \bar{F} \times \mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS} \times m_e) \times N_{\text{nc}} \)

95% Confidence Interval

Closed-form sensitivity analysis using the parameter distributions from Risk Clusters. TCAR is recalculated at ¬±1.96œÉ parameter shifts for Œ≤, k, and recovery rate.

Confidence Bounds $$\text{TCAR}_{95\%} = \left[\text{TCAR}(\theta_{\text{opt}}),\ \text{TCAR}(\theta_{\text{pes}})\right]$$
Optimistic bound (lower TCAR):
\( \beta_{\text{opt}} = \max(1.0,\ \bar{\beta} - 1.96\sigma_\beta) \),   \( k_{\text{opt}} = \max(0.5,\ \bar{k} - 1.96\sigma_k) \)
\( r_{\text{opt}} = \min(0.9,\ \bar{r} + 1.96\sigma_r) \) ‚Äî higher recovery = lower loss

Pessimistic bound (higher TCAR):
\( \beta_{\text{pes}} = \min(3.5,\ \bar{\beta} + 1.96\sigma_\beta) \),   \( k_{\text{pes}} = \min(8.0,\ \bar{k} + 1.96\sigma_k) \)
\( r_{\text{pes}} = \max(0.2,\ \bar{r} - 1.96\sigma_r) \) ‚Äî lower recovery = higher loss
Note: If custom Œ≤ or k overrides are set by the tenant, CI is disabled (bounds = base TCAR) since the custom values represent the user's expert judgment, not a statistical distribution.

Per-BU Segmented Aggregation

For multi-business-unit organizations, ERQF computes risk at BU level then aggregates with cross-BU correlation and brand contagion effects.

Per-BU TCAR $$\text{TCAR}_{\text{bu}} = \text{ERL}_{\text{bu}} + \text{EBI}_{\text{bu}} + \text{RFE}_{\text{bu}}$$
Each BU uses its own:
‚Ä¢ Revenue weight \( w_{\text{bu}} \) ‚Äî fraction of total revenue
‚Ä¢ Cluster parameters ‚Äî BU can override Œ≤, k, avgFine or inherit from org
‚Ä¢ P(Fraud) ‚Äî computed from BU's product categories' scan data
‚Ä¢ Coverage ‚Äî \( \text{BU scans} / (\text{estimated units} \times w_{\text{bu}} / 12) \)
Group Aggregation with Contagion $$\text{TCAR}_{\text{group}} = \sum_i \text{TCAR}_i + \mathcal{C}_{\text{contagion}} - \mathcal{D}_{\text{group}}$$
Brand Contagion (Branded House only):
\( \mathcal{C} = \gamma \times \sum_i P(\text{Fraud})_i \times \left(V_{\text{brand}} - V_{\text{brand}} \times w_i\right) \times \text{IE}_i \)
\( \gamma \) ‚Äî contagion factor (0 = no contagion, 1 = full contagion)

Group Diversification:
\( \mathcal{D}_{\text{group}} = \rho_{\text{cross}} \times 0.3 \times \min(\text{ERL}_{\text{group}}, \text{EBI}_{\text{group}}) \)
\( \rho_{\text{cross}} \) ‚Äî cross-BU correlation (default: 0.3)

Platform ROI Intelligence

v2.1 ¬∑ New

Business-layer metrics quantifying the economic return of the TrustChecker platform. These formulas sit on top of the ERQF core engine (¬ß1‚Äì¬ß10) and translate risk quantification into actionable financial metrics for executive decision-making.

Detection Value $$\text{DV} = N_{\text{counterfeit}} \times \bar{V}_{\text{unit}}$$
Where:
\( N_{\text{counterfeit}} \) ‚Äî total counterfeits detected (all-time)
\( \bar{V}_{\text{unit}} = \frac{R_{\text{annual}}}{U_{\text{estimated}}} \) ‚Äî average unit value
\( U_{\text{estimated}} \) ‚Äî estimated units sold YTD (configured), or \( N_{\text{totalScans}} \) as fallback

Interpretation: Each counterfeit detected prevents ~1 unit of revenue loss at its average value.
Revenue Protected $$R_{\text{protected}} = R_{\text{annual}} \times \underbrace{\frac{N_{\text{counterfeit}} + N_{\text{suspicious}}}{N_{\text{totalScans}}}}_{\text{Fraud Rate}} \times \underbrace{\frac{N_{\text{authentic}}}{N_{\text{totalScans}}}}_{\text{Auth Rate}}$$
Where:
\( R_{\text{protected}} \) ‚Äî estimated fraud loss avoided through detection
Fraud Rate √ó Revenue = total revenue exposed to fraud risk
Auth Rate = platform's detection effectiveness (typically 95%+)
Manual Cost Savings $$S_{\text{manual}} = N_{\text{totalScans}} \times C_{\text{manual}}$$
Where:
\( C_{\text{manual}} \) ‚Äî cost of manual verification per check (default: $5 USD)
Interpretation: Automated scanning replaces manual inspection, saving \( C_{\text{manual}} \) per scan.
ROI Multiple $$\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{DV} + S_{\text{manual}}}{C_{\text{platform}}}$$
Where:
\( C_{\text{platform}} \) ‚Äî annual platform cost (default: $6,000 USD)
\( \text{DV} \) ‚Äî Detection Value (counterfeits √ó unit value)
\( S_{\text{manual}} \) ‚Äî manual verification cost savings

Example: DV = $970K, manual savings = $350K, platform cost = $6K ‚Üí ROI = 220√ó
Payback Period $$T_{\text{payback}} = \left\lceil \frac{C_{\text{platform}}}{(\text{DV} + S_{\text{manual}}) \;/\; M_{\text{active}}} \right\rceil \text{ months}$$
Where:
\( M_{\text{active}} \) ‚Äî number of months the platform has been active
Monthly value = total value generated √∑ months active
Payback = platform cost √∑ monthly value (ceiling to nearest month)
Cost per Detection $$\text{CPD} = \frac{C_{\text{platform}}}{N_{\text{counterfeit}} + N_{\text{suspicious}}}$$
Metric Formula Typical Range
Detection Value counterfeits √ó (revenue / units) $100K ‚Äì $5M
Revenue Protected revenue √ó fraud_rate √ó auth_rate $500K ‚Äì $50M
Cost Savings total_scans √ó manual_cost $50K ‚Äì $500K
ROI Multiple (DV + savings) / platform_cost 10√ó ‚Äì 500√ó
Cost per Detection platform_cost / detections $0.50 ‚Äì $10
Payback Period cost / monthly_value 1 ‚Äì 6 months
Dependency on Financial Inputs: ROI metrics require tenant-configured financial inputs (Annual Revenue, Estimated Units, Platform Cost, Manual Cost per Check). If unconfigured, the system uses scan count as a proxy for unit volume and $6,000 as default platform cost.
