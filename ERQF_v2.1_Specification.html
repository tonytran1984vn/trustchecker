Fraud Probability Model — P(Fraud)

v2.1 · Dynamic Decay

Probability of fraud is computed using an adaptive exponential time-decay weighting. Instead of a static decay rate, v2.1 introduces a volatility-adjusted rate (\(\lambda_{adj}\)), ensuring the system "forgets" older data faster during periods of highly volatile fraud spikes, making anomalies surface immediately.

Primary Formula $$P(\text{Fraud}) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot f_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{D} w_i \cdot n_i}$$
Dynamic Decay · v2.1 $$\lambda_{adj} = \lambda_{base} \cdot (1 + \sigma_{\text{fraud}})$$
Where:
\( w_i = e^{-\lambda_{adj} \cdot d_i} \) — dynamic exponential decay weight for day \(i\)
\( \lambda_{base} = 0.05 \) — base decay constant
\( \sigma_{\text{fraud}} \) — Volatility (std dev) of fraud anomaly rates over the last 7 days
\( d_i \) — number of days ago for day \(i\)
\( f_i = \text{counterfeit}_i + \text{suspicious}_i \times C_r \) — weighted fraud count per day
\( n_i \) — total scans on day \(i\)
Confirmation Rate $$C_r = \frac{\text{Critical Alerts} + \text{High Alerts}}{\text{Total Alerts}}$$

Trend Detection: Compare recent 7-day fraud rate vs prior 7-day rate:

\( R_{\text{recent}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{0..7}}{\text{total}_{0..7}} \),   \( R_{\text{older}} = \frac{\text{fraud}_{8..14}}{\text{total}_{8..14}} \)

If \( R_{\text{recent}} > 1.2 \times R_{\text{older}} \) → Worsening (+1)
If \( R_{\text{recent}} < 0.8 \times R_{\text{older}} \) → Improving (−1)
Otherwise → Stable (0)

Expected Revenue Loss — ERL

Quantifies direct revenue at risk from fraud-related product diversion, counterfeiting losses, and channel leakage. Adjusted by scan coverage ratio to extrapolate from observed to total estimated volume.

Primary Formula $$\text{ERL} = R_{\text{covered}} \times P(\text{Fraud}) \times (1 - r_{\text{recovery}})$$
Where:
\( R_{\text{covered}} = R_{\text{annual}} \times \text{Coverage} \) — revenue covered by scan network
\( \text{Coverage} = \min\!\left(\frac{\text{totalScans}}{\text{estimatedUnits} / 12},\ 1\right) \) — monthly scan penetration rate
\( r_{\text{recovery}} \) — expected recovery rate (default: 0.2 = 20%)
\( 1 - r_{\text{recovery}} \) = Severity factor

Expected Brand Impact — EBI

v2.1 · Integrated Feedback Loop

Models brand value at risk using a power-law trust decay. In v2.1, we acknowledge the interconnected relationship between direct losses and organizational capacity to protect the brand. Trust (\(T\)) naturally degrades as ERL increases in the prior epoch.

Primary Formula $$\text{EBI} = \left[V_{\text{brand}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - \frac{T}{100}\right)^\beta}_{\text{Brand Risk Factor}} \times \underbrace{\left(1 - e^{-k \cdot P(\text{Fraud})}\right)}_{\text{Incident Escalation}}\right] + \mathcal{W}_{\text{mcap}}$$
Market Cap Wipeout (Public Co. Only) $$\mathcal{W}_{\text{mcap}} = V_{\text{mcap}} \times \min(P(\text{Fraud}) \cdot \beta,\ 0.05)$$
Where:
\( V_{\text{brand}} \) — estimated brand value (USD)
\( V_{\text{mcap}} \) — market capitalization of public company
\( T \) — current dynamic trust score (0–100), influenced by recent ERL
\( \beta \) — brand sensitivity exponent (from Risk Cluster, range 1.2–2.8)
\( k \) — incident escalation rate (from Risk Cluster, range 1.25–2.8)

Regulatory Fine Exposure — RFE

v2.0 · Sigmoid

Estimates regulatory fine exposure using a sigmoid enforcement probability that replaces the previous 3-step cliff function, providing smooth risk transition without artificial discontinuities.

Primary Formula $$\text{RFE} = \text{WCRS} \times \bar{F}_{\text{industry}} \times \mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) \times N_{\text{nc}}$$
Compliance Risk Score $$\text{WCRS} = \frac{N_{\text{nc}} \times 1.0 + N_{\text{partial}} \times 0.5}{N_{\text{total}}}$$
Sigmoid Enforcement $$\mathcal{E}(\text{WCRS}) = 0.08 + \frac{0.87}{1 + e^{-12 \cdot (\text{WCRS} - 0.2)}}$$

Supply Chain Risk Index — SCRI

Composite risk index (0–1) combining four risk drivers with cluster-specific weights. Life-critical industries weight compliance higher, while luxury weights fraud/brand higher.

Total Capital at Risk — TCAR

v2.1 · Stress ρ

Aggregates all risk components with correlation-based diversification using portfolio volatility theory. Introducing the Financial Engineering principle: "Correlations go to 1.0 in a crisis". Diversification vanishes when systematic risk spikes.

Primary Formula $$\text{TCAR} = \text{ERL} + \text{EBI} + \text{RFE} - \mathcal{D}$$
Diversification Benefit $$\mathcal{D} = (\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}) \times \left(1 - \sqrt{w_1^2 + w_2^2 + 2\rho_{\text{eff}} \cdot w_1 \cdot w_2}\right)$$
Stress Correlation · v2.1 $$\rho_{\text{eff}} = 0.70 + \frac{0.25}{1 + e^{-15 \cdot (P(\text{Fraud}) - 0.15)}}$$
Where:
\( w_1 = \frac{\text{ERL}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \),   \( w_2 = \frac{\text{EBI}}{\text{ERL} + \text{EBI}} \)
\( \rho_{\text{eff}} \) — effective crisis correlation rating. Aims for 0.70 natively, but scales towards 0.95 when \(P(\text{Fraud})\) exceeds the 15% threshold.
Behavior: As the situation escalates into a public crisis, both revenue and brand sentiment collapse simultaneously, shrinking \( \mathcal{D} \) to near-zero.

Per-BU Segmented Aggregation

For multi-business-unit organizations, ERQF computes individual risk at BU level. Aggregation mechanisms and contagion effects are evaluated cross-BU using the Adjacency Matrix (See Section 11).

Per-BU TCAR $$\text{TCAR}_{\text{bu}} = \text{ERL}_{\text{bu}} + \text{EBI}_{\text{bu}} + \text{RFE}_{\text{bu}}$$

Network Contagion Matrix — Mij

v2.1 · Adaptive Edition

Replaces the simplistic global contagion factor with a deterministic Adjacency Matrix. It maps the DNA of organizational units to quantify how efficiently toxic sentiment or operational risk spills over from one Business Unit (BU \(i\)) to another (BU \(j\)).

Brand Contagion Matrix $$M_{ij} = w_1 \cdot \mathcal{I}_{visual} + w_2 \cdot \mathcal{I}_{customer} + w_3 \cdot \mathcal{I}_{supply}$$
Where:
\( M_{ij} \in [0, 1] \) — exact spillover coefficient. For single BU impact, diagonal \( M_{ii} = 0 \) as self-damage is baked into EBI.
\( \mathcal{I}_{visual} \in \{0, 1\} \) — shared core visual identity/branding (e.g., ParentBrand Corp vs ParentBrand RealEstate). Weight \( w_1 = 0.6 \).
\( \mathcal{I}_{customer} \in [0, 1] \) — target customer demographic overlap ratio. Weight \( w_2 = 0.25 \).
\( \mathcal{I}_{supply} \in [0, 1] \) — shared physical or digital distribution channels. Weight \( w_3 = 0.15 \).
Total Contagion Capital $$\mathcal{C}_{contagion} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{j \neq i}^{N} \underbrace{\left[ P(\text{Fraud})_i \times \text{IE}_i \right]}_{\text{Risk Radiator}} \times \underbrace{V_{\text{brand}, j}}_{\text{Victim Asset}} \times \underbrace{M_{ij}}_{\text{Conductivity}}$$
Interpretation: The magnitude of collateral damage equals the intensity of the localized scandal (Risk Radiator BU \(i\)) multiplied by the exposed capital of the collateral brand (Victim Asset BU \(j\)), perfectly scaled by the network density pathway between them (Conductivity \( M_{ij} \)).
