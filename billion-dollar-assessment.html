Product Core Value

What problem does TrustChecker solve?

TrustChecker makes supply chain trust measurable, auditable, and automated.

In a world where:

  • Supply chains span 15+ countries
  • ESG compliance is mandatory (EU CSRD, US SEC climate rules)
  • Counterfeiting costs $4.2T/year globally
  • A single Bad Supplier can destroy $500M+ in brand value

TrustChecker answers: "Can I trust this supplier? This product? This claim?" — backed by data, not relationships.

Is the value mission-critical?

Yes — but conditionally. Trust scoring is mission-critical only when:

  1. ✅ Procurement teams use it to approve/reject suppliers
  2. ✅ Compliance teams use it for regulatory reporting
  3. ✅ Operations teams use it for incident response
  4. ⚠️ The scores are accurate (fixed today — was broken)
  5. ⚠️ The methodology is transparent and auditable
Verdict: The core value proposition is strong — but TrustChecker must position itself as infrastructure for trust decisions, not just another dashboard.

Does more usage improve the product?

Yes — with cross-org intelligence. When Org A rates Supplier X poorly, and Org B also uses Supplier X, the platform could surface:

  • "3 other organizations flagged this supplier in the last 90 days"
  • "Industry average trust score for this supplier: 0.42"
  • "This supplier's carbon claims are disputed by 2 auditors"

This is the difference between a tool and a network.

Data moat score: 7/10. TrustChecker collects data but keeps it siloed per org. If cross-org intelligence is unlocked, the data moat becomes extremely difficult to replicate.

Network Effects

Current state: No network effects

Each organization uses TrustChecker in isolation. Supplier X's trust score in Org A has no relationship to its score in Org B.

Potential network effects:

Org A rates Supplier X
Org B rates Supplier X
Org C reports incident
↓ ↓ ↓
Network Intelligence Layer
↓ ↓ ↓
All orgs see aggregate risk
Supplier sees reputation
Regulators get risk data

Three tiers of network value:

  1. Cross-org supplier scoring — "Industry consensus" on supplier reliability
  2. Threat intelligence sharing — Anonymized incident/fraud signals across supply chains
  3. Supplier self-service — Suppliers see their own reputation and improve it
Verdict: The architecture has the tables for this, but zero cross-org logic exists today. This is the single most important strategic feature to build.

Market Positioning

Current positioning: Unclear

TrustChecker tries to be a trust scoring platform, supply chain management tool, compliance system, carbon tracking platform, governance framework, and blockchain verification system — all at once.

This is both a strength and a weakness. The breadth shows ambition; the lack of focus dilutes messaging.

Category definition opportunity:

"Supply Chain Trust Intelligence"

No major player owns this category. Competitors exist in supply chain management (SAP Ariba, Oracle), risk intelligence (Moody's, D&B), compliance (SAI360), and ESG reporting (Persefoni) — but none combine trust scoring + supply chain ops + compliance + carbon in one platform.

TrustChecker can own: "The system of record for supply chain trust"

Billion-Dollar Potential

Could TrustChecker become a billion-dollar SaaS company?

Yes — with a specific and narrow path. The ingredients exist:

  • ✅ Large addressable market ($40B+ across supply chain risk, RegTech, ESG)
  • ✅ Physical lock-in moat (QR codes on products)
  • ✅ High switching costs (years of trust data)
  • ✅ Usage-based expansion (more products = more revenue)
  • ✅ Regulatory tailwinds (ESG mandates, supply chain due diligence laws)

But the path requires discipline, not breadth.
